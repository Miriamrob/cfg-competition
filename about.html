Negative attitudes about age can begin to form among children as young as six years old.

Age segregation is a huge problem for the UK and is only getting worse. Generational divides are contributing to unprecedented levels of loneliness and leave older people isolated and institutionalised.

InCommon bring older people and children together to bridge generational divides.

We run activities between primary school students and communities of older people to facilitate experiential learning and combat isolation. Our sessions offer older people the chance to help children improve life skills and confidence through learning, sharing and storytelling.
